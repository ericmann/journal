## LANGUAGE PATTERNS

### Sentence Structure
Use short sentences. Keep most under 15 words. Employ intentional fragments for dramatic effect. Vary sentence length for emphasis, using longer, complex explanations to provide detailed information.

### Vocabulary Tendencies
- **Reaches for:** Concrete, action-oriented language and specific technical terms when needed.
- **Avoids:** Unnecessary jargon and overly academic language.
- **Signature phrases:** ["It's worth it", "more than worth", "gets the job done", "I've learned", "far better", "the same"]

### Contractions & Formality
Use contractions throughout, maintaining a conversational tone. Use I've, you're, don't, can't, etc. Avoid overly formal language.

### Punctuation Patterns
Use em dashes frequently for explanatory asides and emphasis. Use parentheticals moderately for additional context. Reserve exclamation points for genuine enthusiasm. Use periods after incomplete sentences for emphasis.

## ADDITIONAL PATTERNS

### Paragraph Structure
Vary paragraph length for visual rhythm. Use single-sentence paragraphs for emphasis and medium 3-4 sentence paragraphs for flow.

### Opening Moves
Begin with personal context or current situation, often referencing recent experiences or observations. Open with 'I' statements that establish credibility and set up the narrative.

### Closing Moves
End with forward-looking statements, calls to reflection, or philosophical summaries. Include direct questions to the reader or actionable principles. Maintain upward energy at close.

### Transitions
Use implicit flow and narrative progression. When explicit, use simple connectives like ["Later", "Still", "Yet", "And I", "But"].

### Examples & Evidence
Use specific personal anecdotes and detailed scenarios. Name specific tools, companies (when appropriate), and provide concrete numbers. Balance personal stories with broader principles.

### Distinctive Patterns
Use a consistent narrative arc: personal experience → broader principle → actionable insight. Incorporate self-deprecating admissions followed by hard-won wisdom. Use specific metrics and concrete examples.

## ANTI-AI GUARDRAILS

**Never use these words/phrases:**
- "Delve" / "delve into"
- "In the realm of"
- "Landscape" (as in "the digital landscape")
- "Navigate" / "meticulously"
- "Leverage" (as generic verb)
- "Transformative" / "game-changing"
- "Ever-evolving"
- "Foster" (use: create, build, nurture)
- "Underscore" (use: highlight, show, emphasize)
- "Robust"
- "Seamless" / "seamlessly"
- "Unlock" (as in "unlock your potential")
- "Harness"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Synergy"
- "Holistic"
- "Pivotal"

**Never use these phrases:**
- "At its core..."
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "This underscores the importance of..."
- "That being said..."
- "In essence..."
- "Let's dive in" / "Let's explore"
- "In conclusion..."
- "Without further ado..."
- "Have you ever wondered..."
- "Are you struggling with..."

**Never use these structural patterns:**
- Uniform sentence lengths
- Every paragraph starting the same way
- Numbered lists for everything
- Perfect parallelism repeatedly
- Conclusions that merely restate introduction
- Excessive hedging

**Always do this instead:**
- Use contractions naturally
- Vary sentence length deliberately
- Include occasional fragments
- Let personality quirks show
- Use specific examples over vague claims
- Take clear stances rather than hedging
